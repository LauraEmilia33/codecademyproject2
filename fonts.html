Fonts

There are many different ways to choose your fonts for a site. My favorite thing to do is use a tool called "WhatFont" which allows you to pick fonts from a website. I also love to have a browse in Google Fonts as they are always updating it and releasing new and different styles of fonts. Normally when I design a website I first think of a theme and then the font follows. For example with this project, I thought of doing a "chalkboard" theme. That meant that the font should be cursive and that it almost looked like handwriting. I ended up going for this wonderful font you see in the heading called "Spline Sans Mono" in Google fonts. Below is the two tools I use for fonts, and some of my favorite fonts at the moment.

1. WhatFont

As I mentioned before, this tool allows you to select ANY font on a website and see what the font is. I do this even when just browsing through the internet and think, "Wow, what a font" and just save it for later use. Trust me, it's a must!

2. Google Fonts

Google fonts has a range of different fonts you can use and simply import into your code. You simply select font you want and you can copy the code into your html code. They even have the option to write something to see how your text might look like, and even the weight of the fonts. For this site I imported the header font and the font I use for my paragraphs.
